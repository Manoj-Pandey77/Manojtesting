About MBBS

MBBS Full Form

 

MBBS full form is Bachelor of Medicine and Bachelor of Surgery. It is a 5 year and 6 month undergraduate double degree medical education necessary for those wishing to become doctors. The MBBS Course is a professional degree necessary to practise medicine, diagnose ailments, prescribe medication, and perform surgery. The abbreviation MBBS comes from the Latin phrase Medicinae Baccalaureus Baccalaureus Chirurgiae.

The NEET test is used to determine MBBS admission. To qualify for the NEET Exam, students must be over the age of 17 and have completed Physics, Chemistry, Mathematics, and Biology as required subjects in class 12. NEET 2022 is scheduled to take place in September 2022.

The MBBS fees ranges between INR 5,00,000 and INR 50,00,000, and those who successfully complete MBBS have various career prospects following MBBS. Candidates with an MBBS degree can work as doctors in private and public hospitals, railroads, and the military services. For better career opportunities, students can pursue postgraduate medical degrees such as MD Medicine or study an MBA after completing their MBBS.

MBBS Eligibility Criteria

 

·       To get admission into MBBS programme in India, students have to complete their 10+2 education with Physics, Chemistry & Biology and score minimum 50% marks (40% in the case of reserved category).

·       Student's age should be between 17 years to 25 years.

·       The given qualification is not enough to get admission into MBBS course, students have to appear for the National Level Examination conducted by the CBSE, New Delhi, i.e. NEET 2022 (National Eligibility Cum Entrance Test).

·       From the year 2016, the Supreme Court bans the state level or university level examinations for admission into MBBS & BDS courses.

·       Now, all the admission into MBBS course is through NEET Exam.

MBBS Entrance Exam 2022

NEET is required for MBBS admission.If a student is interested in MBBS admission, he/she can apply to any of India's medical institutions in one of three ways:

  • NEET - for admission to all private and government medical institutes in India for MBBS.
  • JIPMER - Only for admission to JIPMER, Pondicherry.
  • AIIMS - Only for admission to AIIMS Delhi.

 

Prior to 2016, each state in India had its own admission test. Many private colleges have their own entrance exams as ell.

Following the Supreme Court's ruling, a single admission test, NEET, was created to replace all of these entrance examinations.You may apply for admission to practically all medical institutions in India using this method.

Currently, JIPMER Pondicherry and AIIMS Delhi have the authority to hold their own entrance exams in addition to the NEET.There are just three entrance tests in India for MBBS admission.More than 15 lakh candidates take the NEET entrance test for MBBS admission each year.It is critical to perform well on this admission exam.Following the NEET test, the results are issued, along with a cut off point over which only candidates can be admitted to the MBBS or BDS programme.

 

The NEET test has a total score of 700, and if a student scores over 400, he may expect to get into a reputable college. For Admission into Government Colleges one must score very well in NEET.

MBBS Admission 2022: Tips to Crack the Exam

 

To crack the NEET examination is not so easy. You must start preparing for the NEET examination one/two years before the examination will be scheduled. Below are some important preparation tips to crack the admission to pursue an MBBS course:-

  • Follow a timetable: Prepare a timetable and try to follow that timetable till you appear for the NEET examination.
  • Cover all the basics of each subject: To know the basics of each subject is very much important. Especially the basics of subjects like Physics, Chemistry, and Biology papers. As questions are asked mainly from these subjects.
  • Study Material: Gather the correct NEET study material such as books, sample papers, question papers, and notes.
  • Do regular practice: Cover all the syllabus and do regular practice. Try to collect previous years ‘question papers and find out the most repeated questions. The probabilities of asking the important questions are high which repeats every year.
  • Appear in Mock Test: Attempt several mock tests and time yourself. NEET Mock tests will clear all your confusion regarding the examination and you will learn time management during the NEET examination.
  • Prepare crisp Notes: Make handy notes while preparing for the exam. The notes will be very helpful for revision.
  • Solve Previous Year Question Paper: Solve the previous year's NEET Question papers and NEET sample papers to have a clear understanding of the exam pattern and type of questions asked in the exam.

MBBS Subjects and Syllabus

 

MBBS Subjects include gross anatomy, Osteology, Muscular system, Arthrology, Cardiovascular System, Respiratory System, Digestive System, Genito-Urinary System, Endocrine System and Individual Endocrine Glands, Nervous System and its components etc. The year wise MBBS Subjects and Syllabus are mentioned below

 

MBBS 1st year subjects

 

MBBS subjects in the first year consist of Anatomy, Biochemistry, and Physiology.

 

MBBS Subjects

Description

Anatomy

Microanatomy, Embryology and Genetics, Gross anatomy, and Neuroanatomy.

Biochemistry

Molecular Biology, Biological cell, Hormones, Metabolic pathways, Enzymes, Cancer and cancer makers, Biomolecules, Food assimilation and nutrition.

Physiology

Respiratory System, Gastrointestinal System, Nutrition, General Physiology, Kidney, Nerve–Muscle, Neurophysiology, Blood, Environmental Physiology, Cardiovascular System, Nutrition, Yoga.

 

 

2nd year MBBS subjects

 

MBBS subjects in the second year consist of community medicine, pharmacology, pathology, microbiology, forensic medicine and toxicology, clinical postings and OPD.

 

MBBS Subjects

Description

Community Medicine

Infestations, Infective dermatoses, Ineffective dermatoses, Allergic disorders, Allergic disorders, Melanin synthesis, urticaria, Epidermopoiesis, Pathogenesis, Psoriasis, Gonococcal and Nongonococcal infections, HIV infection, Drug eruptions, Dermatological Emergencies, Vesiculo-bullous diseases, erythema multiforme,

Pharmacology

General Pharmacology, Autacoids, Gastrointestinal and respiratory system, Cardiovascular system, Hormones, Miscellaneous, Central nervous system, Autonomic nervous system & The peripheral nervous system, Chemotherapy,

Pathology

Systemic Pathology, General Pathology, Practical.

Microbiology

Bacteriology, Microbiology, Virology, Laboratory Diagnosis of Viral Infection, Common Tests for Bacterial identification, Common Laboratory Methods for Diagnosis of Fungal Infections, Bacterial and Viral Genetics, Collection of Transport of Samples, Microorganisms associated with gastrointestinal infections, Sterilisation and disinfection, Gastrointestinal infections caused by parasites, Mycology, parasitology, Vaccines, Host-Parasite relationship, Immunodiagnosis, Bacterial Staining and Cultivation,

Forensic Medicine and Toxicology

Toxicology, Forensic Medicine.

Clinical postings in wards

Clinical postings in wards is what every MBBS student has to go through. Here students will get exposed to 12 week of clinical posting in the field of community medicine.

OPD

OPD or Outpatient Department is defined as a particular place in the hospital which consists of medical and physical facilities, along with other staff. Everything takes place here on regularly scheduled hours care to the patients who are not primarily registered as the inpatients.

 

3rd year MBBS subjects

 

MBBS Subjects in 3rd year consist of Community Medicine, ENT, Ophthalmology .

 

MBBS Subjects

Description

Community Medicine

Community Medicine deals with the health system of the overall population of a community. The service provided at Primary Healthcare centres are taught in this subject.

ENT

The full form of ENT is Ear, Nose and Throat. This field of medicine is also known as otolaryngology. The treatment of the ear, nose, throat, head and neck area of the human body is dealt with in ENT subjects.

Ophthalmology

Ophthalmology deals with the treatment and diagnosis of the eye. It is derived from the Greek words ophthalmos meaning “eye” and logia meaning “study of the course”

FAQs

Q-1

What is MBBS full form?


MBBS full form is Bachelor of Medicine and Bachelor of Surgery..
Q-2

What exactly is MBBS course?


Bachelor of Medicine and Bachelor of Surgery popularly known as MBBS is a professional degree to practice in the field of medicine. MBBS syllabus is mandatory to study during their Bachelor's degree for candidates who want to become a doctor..
Q-3

How many marks are required in neet for MBBS?


General category candidates need to score 50 % to qualify. whereas candidates belonging to the OBC category need to score 45% to Qualify NEET..
Q-4

What to do after completing mbbs?


MBBS graduates have long list of career prospects. Some of which are listed below- • Specialisation • Diploma of National Board • International Education • Combined Medical Services • Defence Services • Private and Government Hospital Employment • MBA in the Medical Field • Clinical Research • Start A Clinic.
Q-5

How many MBBS seats are there in India?


As per the information available there are a total 1, 18, 316 MBBS & BDS Seats available in India for NEET 2020. Of them, roughly 80 thousand are MBBS seats which comprise 41,388 Government Medical seats as well as 1107 AIIMS, JIPMER MBBS seats..
Q-6

Is MBBS a difficult to pursue?


The first year of MBBS is challenging and demanding. Though the first year is a lot to take in as a beginner, the syllabus isn't impossible to study; it's simply long, and MBBS becomes tough to cope with if underestimated..
Q-7

What is the average fees of doiing MBBS from a government college?


The cost of an MBBS course may differ between government, private, and deemed universities. The typical cost of doing MBBS from a government college in India is from INR 20,000 to INR 7.5 lakh, whereas the MBBS fee at private universities can range from INR 20 lakh to more than INR 1 Cr..
Q-8

What is the MBBS course duration in India?


The length of the MBBS course is five years and one year of internship in the nonprofit organization, medical centres and rehabilitation centres..
Q-9

How many semester's are there in mbbs?


The complete MBBS curriculum has been separated into nine semesters. Each of the nine semesters will last six months. Each semester will include roughly 120 teaching days..
Q-10

What is the average salary of an MBBS doctor in India?


Mbbs Doctor's salary in India ranges between ₹ 0.5 Lakhs to ₹ 12.0 Lakhs with an average annual salary of ₹ 6.0 Lakhs.
Q-11

How many govt MBBS seats are there in India?


A total of 6,222 MBBS seats are available for All India Counselling out of a total of 41,480 MBBS government college seats, accounting for 15% of the total. These seats will be open to all NEET qualified candidates, with seat allocation based on NEET 2020 All India Rank..
Q-12

Is doing an MBBS worth it?


If that is what you desire, MBBS is well worth it. It is one of the most difficult courses available. Nowadays, it is not enough to have an MBBS; one must also have an MD/MS, a DM/MCH, a few fellowships, and what not. The learning duration for MBBS and MD is about a decade, and 12-13 years if you want to add an MCH to it..
Q-13

Can I do MBBS after earning a degree in Bsc nursing?


Yes, you can get into mbbs after graduating from bsc nursing. To be accepted to MBBS, you must pass the NEET, which is held every year..
Q-14

What is management quota in MBBS?


15% of seats in private medical colleges have a cost structure that is distinct from the standard prescribed fees. The seats with this fee structure can be referred to as Management quota seats or NRI seats..
Q-15

Can I get admission in an mbbs course with a neet score of 300??


Yes, you can get a medical seat if you score 300 on the NEET. However, even if you are from the obc category, your chances of getting into a government college are pretty slim with 300 marks..
